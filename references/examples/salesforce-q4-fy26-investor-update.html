A Defining Year

Fiscal 2026 was a defining year for Salesforce. We delivered $41.5 billion in full-year revenue, up 10% year-over-year, and closed the year with a record fourth quarter that exceeded expectations across every key metric.

Agentforce has reached $800 million in annual recurring revenue, growing 169% year-over-year. We closed over 29,000 deals, with production accounts increasing nearly 50% in Q4. Our platform delivered 2.4 billion Agentic Work Units.

We returned $14.3 billion to shareholders in FY26 and announce a new $50 billion buyback authorization and 5.8% dividend increase.

Marc Benioff

Chair and Chief Executive Officer

Salesforce, Inc.The Agentforce Revolution
Platform Momentum
From Copilots to Autonomous Agents

Since launching in late 2024, Agentforce has closed over 29,000 deals, up 50% quarter-over-quarter. Production accounts increased nearly 50% in Q4. Agentforce ARR reached $800 million, while the broader Agentforce and Data 360 combined ARR exceeded $2.9 billion.

Salesforce introduced Agentic Work Units (AWUs) to measure autonomous tasks. In Q4, AWUs reached 2.4 billion, up 57% QoQ. The platform processed nearly 20 trillion tokens. 75% of top-100 deals included both Agentforce and Data 360.

Looking Ahead
The Path to $63 Billion

For FY27, Salesforce guides revenue of $45.8–$46.2 billion (+10–11%). Q1 FY27 expected at $10.65–$10.70 billion. Non-GAAP operating margin guided to 34.0%.

Subscription growth should remain in the low double digits, driven by Agentforce attach rates and Data 360 expansion. Informatica integration on track for $200M in run-rate synergies by early FY28.

Management reiterated its FY30 target of $63 billion, implying a mid-teens CAGR. The bull case: Agentforce becomes a usage-based revenue engine at scale.
